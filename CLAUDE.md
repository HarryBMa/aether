## Design Context

### Users
This product serves multi-disciplinary clinical teams and patients coordinating complex surgical pathways, especially large surgeries such as lambåkirurgi across multiple teams and care settings.

Primary users include surgeons, nurses, coordinators, and patients. The core job is to make high-stakes care coordination legible, shared, and actionable across planning, imaging, scheduling, decision-making, and patient communication.

### Brand Personality
Clear, easy, functional.

The interface should feel calm and dependable under pressure. It should support serious clinical work without feeling cold or bureaucratic, and it should become more approachable in patient-facing views without losing credibility.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a restrained clinical foundation with a more approachable patient-facing tone where appropriate.

The current direction already establishes a strong base: muted OKLCH neutrals, dense but readable layouts, monospaced metadata, and focused accent colors. Continue that direction, but soften patient-facing moments so they feel more humane and supportive.

Reference influences:
- Milanote: collaborative spatial organization, clear grouping, flexible working surface
- Miro: shared-canvas collaboration, visible team participation, lightweight multi-user affordances

Anti-reference:
- Do not make this look like a generic AI SaaS product

Accessibility target:
- WCAG AA minimum

### Design Principles
1. Calm clarity over visual novelty. Use hierarchy, spacing, and contrast to reduce cognitive load in high-stakes workflows.
2. Coordination first. Design should make people, decisions, timing, and dependencies immediately visible across teams and care instances.
3. Clinician and patient tones should differ, but belong to the same system. Clinical surfaces can be denser and more operational; patient surfaces should be softer, clearer, and more reassuring.
4. Collaboration should feel ambient, not noisy. Presence, annotations, comments, and shared planning tools should support teamwork without overwhelming the interface.
5. Avoid generic AI-product aesthetics. No glossy dashboard clichés, empty futurism, or decorative intelligence cues that weaken trust.
